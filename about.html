About the Band

Origin: Los Angeles, California, U.S.
Genre: Pop rock / Psychedelic pop
Line-up: Peter Tork – bass guitar, vocals, guitar, keyboards, banjo; Micky Dolenz – vocals, drums, percussion, guitar, synthesizers; Davy Jones – vocals, percussion, drums, guitar, piano (died in 2012); Michael Nesmith – guitar, vocals, keyboards, harmonica

The Monkees, an American rock and pop band were formed in Los Angeles in 1965 by Bob Rafelson and Bert Schneider for the American television series The Monkees which aired from 1966 to 1968. The band were very active between 1965 and 1971 with subsequent reunion albums and tours in the decades that followed. There was also "The Monkees" comic published in the United States by Dell Comics, which ran for seventeen issues from 1967 to 1969.

The television show was canceled in 1968, but the band continued to record music through 1971. A revival of interest in the television show in 1986 led to further new records and a series of reunion tours. In the 1990s, The Monkees continued to record new material.

The 45th anniversary tour would be the last with Jones, who died of a heart attack at age 66, on February 29, 2012. Towards the end of 2012, The Monkees embarked on a series of US shows to honour Jones' memory, which were well received by fans. In May 2016, The Monkees released a new album, to celebrate their 50th anniversary entitled Good Times!
